# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Creators and freelancers juggling multiple projects at once. They open Wazheefa when they need to settle into real work — not to manage a team or run a business operation. They value tools that stay out of the way: fast capture, clear priorities, and built-in focus aids (timer, ambient music) without the overhead of signup, sync, or enterprise admin.

## Product Purpose

Wazheefa is a local-first productivity app for entering and sustaining focused work sessions. Users open it, see their tasks clearly, start a timer or music if they want, and get things done — no account, no cloud dependency, no friction between intent and action. Success means a user sits down, enters flow within seconds, and leaves with meaningful progress on what matters.

## Brand Personality

**Calm · Refined · Focused**

Quiet confidence over loud marketing. The interface should feel like a well-made tool that respects attention — precise, unhurried, and trustworthy. Personality shows up in craft and restraint, not decoration or hype.

## Anti-references

- **AI-default aesthetics**: warm cream/sand body backgrounds, gradient text, decorative glassmorphism, identical icon-card grids, uppercase eyebrow kickers on every section
- **Generic SaaS landing patterns**: hero metrics, purple-gradient accents, "startup template" visual grammar
- **Gamified hustle culture**: streaks, badges, confetti, productivity guilt
- **Corporate enterprise heaviness**: Jira/Asana-scale admin chrome, dense dashboards, configuration-first UX

## Design Principles

1. **Focus is the product** — Every screen should help users enter and stay in a work session; secondary features (music, categories) support focus, never compete with it.
2. **Speed to session** — Zero friction from open to working: no signup, local-first, immediate task visibility.
3. **Opinionated simplicity** — Fewer choices, clearer defaults. Inspired by Linear's fast, opinionated craft without copying its visual identity.
4. **Quiet confidence** — Utility without shouting. Hierarchy and spacing do the work; decoration is earned, not default.
5. **Practice what we preach** — The app itself must feel calm and distraction-free. If the UI creates anxiety or noise, it has failed its purpose.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Sensible defaults: keyboard navigation for interactive controls, visible focus states, semantic markup, and `prefers-reduced-motion` respect for animations. No special compliance target beyond standard good practice.
